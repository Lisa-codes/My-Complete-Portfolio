Project Overview

I am a big fan of flowers and always get mine from a specific guy inside the city market so I was inspired to make a website where people could easily order flowers from him. User Research shows also that special events and occasions ignite excitement and thus florist businesses have been booming. In order to get a wide reach, business owners need online visibility and users need a quick and efficient way to make orders without the trouble of having to go physically to their local flower shops during their busy day-to-day lives. This product provides users with an easier way to access them while enhancing the user experience through visual design and smooth user flow to promote high conversion rates.

My Role

Lead UI/UX Designer

Project Duration

This project was done in 1 month from 20th September to 19th October 2023

The Opportunity

In the realm of floral commerce, local florists are grappling with a critical issue: inadequate online visibility. Meanwhile, consumers seek a streamlined solution for placing flower orders swiftly and effortlessly. Presently, competitor websites suffer from substantial drop-off rates, primarily due to fragmented user journeys that necessitate phone calls for order placement, devoid of visual aids. Furthermore, alternative platforms are plagued by overcrowding, resulting in information overload, heightened user error rates, and suboptimal system usability. Addressing these challenges is imperative to enhance the online presence of local florists and provide users with an intuitive ordering experience that fosters satisfaction and loyalty.

The Solution

To address these challenges, I designed a flower ordering website where users could easily access or order their flowers without getting to the physical location. This will enhance local florists online visibility thus giving them a competitive edge and expanding audience reach that also promotes seamless user experience, promoting high conversion rates and satisfaction among diverse users.

User Personas

During the empathize stage i created two user personas based on real people who use this service. The personas present users motivations and goals which helped in crafting the user journey while addressing initial pain points of the actual users .

User Flow Diagram

In the Flower City Website , the user flow begins with the Sign-Up process, where users are prompted to create an account by entering their basic information, such as name, email, and password, or by signing in using third-party authentication options like Google or Email. Once signed up, users proceed to the Home Screen, which presents popular products and the home page menu at the top that has links to:product page, about page, contact page and an option to sign out. For users to add an item to cart they can click the CTA button showing 'View all products' and be redirected to the prodcut page. On this page they can click on an item, view the item details and add item to cart. Then proceed to the checkout page where they can verify the payment method , complete payment and finally land on the thank you for your purchase page where they can be redirected to the home page to restart the user journey.

Competitive Analysis

The secondary research showed that users did not like websites whose landing pages were overcrowded with information and images as they portrayed poor information architecture. Users also appreciated websites they could navigate back and forth easily and complete the checkout process without having to call the business owners. This allowed me to use and apply these findings to my own design to enhance user experience.

User Research

The method of user research applied to this project was qualitative and secondary research based on already-developed product websites. Users responded to the websites which had a simple and smooth user flow and whose visual designs were appealing,Pain points:

  • Poor Information Architecture
  • Complicated User Flow
  • Poor Navigational Cues

Affinity Mapping

After conducting the first usbaility study, i created an affinity map based on the insights gained from the users and found common pain points that i used during the design iteration

  • 25% were unable to complete their user journey on mobile devices .
  • 50% of users noted that not all items were clickable hence causing an incomplete user journey.
  • 25% of the users mentioned on the quantity CTA functionality on view items page was not functional.

Takeaways & Future Considerations

During the usability study I conducted the common theme was that users found navigation to be smooth and easy and the flow from home page to checkout was very clear. I learned that accessibility is very important and thus made all fonts clear by arranging them in a hierarchical form. What I changed was the transitioning from one page to another to allow less time for time on task. In future projects, I would add two sections in the header to peak intrinsic interest in users and use more secondary colors in the background instead of a plain white to enhance visual design
